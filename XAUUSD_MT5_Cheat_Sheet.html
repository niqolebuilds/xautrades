Gold (XAUUSD) · MetaTrader 5 Cheat Sheet

Keep next to you while practicing on a DEMO account. Gold ~$4,050/oz (Jul 2026 — verify live). Not financial advice.

Contract math (memorize this)

Lot sizeOunces$1 gold move =
1.00 (standard)100 oz$100
0.10 (mini)10 oz$10
0.01 (micro)1 oz$1

Leverage (1:100, 1:500) only changes margin frozen, NOT your risk per move. Size off risk, never off margin.

Place an order (MT5)

  1. Market Watch → find XAUUSD (may be GOLD)
  2. Right-click → Chart Window
  3. New Order (F9)
  4. Set Volume (start 0.01)
  5. Set Stop Loss + Take Profit BEFORE clicking
  6. Buy = expect up   Sell = expect down

Order types

  • Market — fills now at current price
  • Buy/Sell Limit — fills at a better price you set
  • Buy/Sell Stop — fills once price breaks a level (breakout)
  • SL — auto-closes at your max loss (never skip)
  • TP — auto-closes at your profit target

Position-size formula

Risk$ = lots × 100 × stop_distance$
lots = Risk$ ÷ (100 × stop_distance$)

Stop distance = dollars of gold price between entry and SL.

At 0.01 lot: every $1 of stop distance = $1 of risk. Use the calculator to size trades.

Worked example (gold ~$4,050)

You Buy 0.01 lot at 4,050, SL $10 below (4,040), TP $20 above (4,070).

  • Hit TP → +$20   |   Hit SL → −$10   (2:1 reward-to-risk)
  • But −$10 = 20% of a $50 account. Two losers ≈ account crippled.
  • Lesson: correct risk (1–2% per trade) is mathematically impossible on $50 with gold's smallest lot.

The 6 non-negotiable rules

  1. Demo first — weeks, not hours.
  2. Never open a trade without a Stop Loss.
  3. Risk a fixed % per trade (aim 1–2%).
  4. Reward ≥ 2× risk, or skip it.
  5. Watch spread + overnight swap fees — they eat small accounts.
  6. One trade at a time while learning.

Login checklist

  • Account is with a broker, not "MetaTrader."
  • Need 3 things: Server, Login #, Password.
  • Server name must match the broker's email exactly.
  • Symbol suffixes vary: XAUUSD, XAUUSD.m, GOLD.
Reality check: ~70–80% of retail traders lose money with proper capital. At $50 you've removed all margin for error — gold's normal daily swing ($30–80 at $4,050) can wipe a micro-lot position. Treat this first $50 as tuition, not capital. A realistically-funded gold account for sane 1–2% risk starts closer to $500–2,000.
