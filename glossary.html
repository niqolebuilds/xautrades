Trading Glossary

The vocabulary, in plain English. Type to filter.

Order & execution

Long / Short
Long = you bought, profit if price rises. Short = you sold first, profit if price falls.
Bid / Ask
Bid = price you can sell at; Ask = price you can buy at. Ask is always higher.
Spread
The gap between bid and ask — the broker's built-in cost. On gold, ~20–40¢ normally, but it blows out around news.
Slippage
When your order fills at a worse price than expected because the market moved in the split-second it took to execute.
Market / Limit / Stop order
Market = fill now. Limit = fill only at a better price. Stop = fill once price breaks a trigger level.
Stop-loss (SL) / Take-profit (TP)
Auto-close orders. SL caps your loss; TP locks your profit. Set both before entering.

Size, risk & money

Lot
The trade size unit. For gold, 1.00 lot = 100 oz; 0.01 lot (micro) = 1 oz. A $1 gold move = $1 per 0.01 lot.
Pip / Point (gold)
For XAUUSD a "pip" is usually a $0.10 move; a "point" often a $0.01 move. Brokers differ — verify. Easiest to just think in dollars of price.
Leverage
Borrowing from the broker to control a larger position. 1:500 means $1 controls $500 of gold. Amplifies gains AND losses.
Margin
The deposit frozen to hold a leveraged position. Run out and you get a margin call / auto-liquidation.
Swap / Rollover
A fee (or credit) for holding a position overnight. Small per night, but it grinds down small accounts held for days.
Drawdown
The peak-to-trough drop in your account. A 50% drawdown needs a 100% gain just to recover.
R / R-multiple
Your result measured in units of risk. Risk $10, make $20 = +2R. Lets you compare trades of different sizes.
Reward : Risk (R:R)
Potential profit vs. potential loss on a trade. Aim for 2:1 or better.
Expectancy
Average profit per trade over many trades. Positive expectancy + enough trades = you make money even with a sub-50% win rate.

Market behavior

Volatility / ATR
How much price moves. ATR (Average True Range) quantifies the typical range per period — use it to size stops sensibly.
Liquidity
How easily you can trade without moving price. High during London–NY overlap; thin in the Asian session.
Scalping
Very short-term trading — seconds to minutes — taking small, frequent profits. Costs (spread) matter enormously.
Whipsaw / Fake-out
Price breaks a level, sucks traders in, then sharply reverses — stopping them out. Common around news.
ATH
All-Time High — the highest price ever. Gold's was ~$5,595 (Jan 2026 in this dataset).

Gold-specific macro drivers

DXY (Dollar Index)
The US dollar vs. a basket of currencies. Gold usually moves inverse to DXY — strong dollar, weak gold.
Real yields / US10Y
Inflation-adjusted bond yields. Rising real yields = headwind for gold (which pays no interest); falling = tailwind.
CPI / PCE
Inflation reports. Hot inflation → hawkish Fed → often pressures gold; soft inflation → the reverse. High-impact, high-volatility releases.
NFP
Non-Farm Payrolls — the monthly US jobs report. One of the biggest scheduled volatility events; spreads widen, expect whipsaw.
FOMC
The Fed's rate-setting meetings. Rate decisions and the chair's tone move the dollar and gold hard.
VWAP
Volume-Weighted Average Price — an intraday "fair value" line many scalpers use as a reference level.
